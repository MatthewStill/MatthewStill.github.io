Method & Findings

  • Data: FAA wildlife strike records with date, airport, state, time of day, species class, damage indicator, and cost.
  • Modeling: Measures for total strikes, cost per strike, strikes by class and time of day; page-level filters for state and airport.
  • Visuals: Filled map and bubble map, bar charts by airport, time-of-day column chart, yearly trend, species share pie, and damage share pie.
  • Findings: Strikes are mostly bird-related and occur in daytime; counts concentrate in large-traffic states and hubs; 89%+ of cases report no damage, yet a small set of airports drive a large share of total cost; long-run costs show volatility with recent moderation.
  • Actions: Prioritize bird-hazard mitigation at high-volume hubs, schedule sweeps for daytime peaks, and target airports with outsized cost exposure.
